## Context

What situation forces a decision? Facts only: constraints, requirements, the state of the
platform. A reader with zero context must understand the situation from this section alone.

## Problem

The specific question being decided, in one or two sentences. If it can't be stated as a
question, it isn't a decision.

## Alternatives considered

For each alternative seriously considered (including "do nothing" where meaningful):

### Option A — Name

- Summary of the approach
- Pros
- Cons
- Why (not) chosen

### Option B — Name

…

## Decision

The chosen option, stated unambiguously, with the decisive reasons. Prescriptive, not
descriptive: "We will…", not "We could…".

## Pros

What this decision buys us.

## Cons

What we knowingly give up. An ADR with no cons is advertising, not engineering.

## Consequences

What becomes true after this decision: new constraints, required follow-up work, things that are
now easier/harder. Include consequences for future decisions.

## Operational impact

What changes in day-to-day operations: procedures, runbooks affected, monitoring, maintenance
burden.

## Security considerations

Attack surface, secrets handling, exposure, blast radius. Write "None identified" only after
actually thinking about it.

## Future review

The conditions under which this decision must be re-examined (e.g. "when node count > 1",
"at Mini PC migration", "if project X becomes unmaintained") — conditions, not dates, where
possible.
